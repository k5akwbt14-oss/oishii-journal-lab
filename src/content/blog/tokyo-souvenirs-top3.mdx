Last day in Tokyo, a wall of 200+ boxed sweets at the station, and a train to catch. Which one do you grab?

You don't need to try them all. Locals reach for the same few **omiyage** (souvenir gifts) every time — they travel well and never disappoint. Here are the top three.

## Buying tips before you check out

<Callout title="🧳 Don't let a good souvenir go to waste">
- **Buy last, not first.** All three are best within a few days — grab them on your way to the airport.
- **Airport = city price.** Haneda and Narita carry the same boxes for the same price. No rush.
- **Ask for an ice pack (`horeizai`) in summer** for cream-based sweets — most shops give one free.
- **Check the shelf life (`shōmi kigen`)** if it needs to last more than 2–3 days.
</Callout>

<InaripoBubble expression="wink">
Insider tip: buy your sweets *last*, right before your flight. Same airport price, freshest when you land. 😉
</InaripoBubble>

Can only carry one? Let the occasion decide: Tokyo Banana for the crowd, Sweet Potato Tamago for the one picky friend, Sugar Butter Sand no Ki for the whole office.
