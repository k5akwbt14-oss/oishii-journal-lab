## What actually shows up

Every Bokksu Classic box includes:

- **20–22 Japanese snacks and sweets** — many from small regional makers you won't easily find outside Japan
- **A tea of the month** — rotating monthly, from matcha to *hojicha* (roasted green tea)
- **A 20–24 page culture guide** — where each item comes from, tasting notes, and allergen info
- **A monthly theme** — one story that ties the whole box together (a region, a season, a tradition)

The culture guide is what quietly sets Bokksu apart. It turns a bag of mystery snacks into something you can actually understand — which matters a lot if you're new to Japanese sweets, or giving the box as a gift.

At **roughly $33–40 for 20–22 items**, you're paying **about $1.60–2.00 per snack**. That's more than a convenience-store haul — but you're paying for hard-to-find imports that come with a guide, not another bag of the usual suspects.

## Pros and cons

**The good**
- Snacks from small regional producers, not just the big classic brands
- The culture guide adds real context (which also makes it a great gift)
- A themed selection keeps every month fresh
- Prices drop meaningfully on longer plans

**The not-so-good**
- Higher cost per item than stocking up yourself
- You can't pick the items — it's a hand-picked surprise (a pro for some, a con for others)
- The wrong box if you only want the famous classics

## Who should subscribe

<Callout title="Bokksu is a great fit if you…">
- are **new to Japanese snacks** and want a guided, authentic first bite
- want a thoughtful **gift** that arrives with its own story
- enjoy the **surprise** of a chosen box more than picking everything yourself
- care about **regional, hard-to-find** makers, not just the big names
</Callout>

<Callout title="Look elsewhere if you…">

- only want the **classics** you can already buy
