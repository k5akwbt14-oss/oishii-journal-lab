## What Is TokyoTreat?

TokyoTreat ships Japanese snacks directly from Japan every month, on **a single plan** — roughly **12–13 items per box**, plus a full-color guidebook explaining that month's theme.

**Every issue has a theme.** Here's the recent run:

Four consecutive boxes, four distinct themes. That's the tell: this isn't leftover inventory in a box — it's built monthly, more like a **magazine issue** than a grab bag. Shipping is worldwide, and you can cancel anytime.

## Who Subscribes to This?

Judging from what the boxes contain, four reader profiles fit:

- **KitKat flavor chasers.** A Japan-exclusive full-size KitKat bag has appeared in **six straight boxes** (details below).
- **Families.** A microphone-shaped candy toy, *ramune* (fizzy soda-flavored candy) with a mystery toy, DIY sushi gummies — most boxes include at least one **DIY candy kit** (make-it-yourself candy from powder and water). And 12–13 items splits cleanly among a few people.
- **Homesick expats.** The lineup includes instant noodles alongside the sweets — familiar shelf staples for anyone who grew up in Japan.
- **Gift-givers.** A dedicated gift plan turns it into a recurring present. (Note: the gift plan is excluded from the KIRBY26 campaign below.)

## When TokyoTreat Isn't Your Box

TokyoTreat isn't the right call for everyone:

- **Chasing one specific product?** A specialty shop like Japan Candy Store is the saner route — though their range covers only a slice of what circulates in Japan.
- **Want quiet, traditional *wagashi* (refined Japanese tea sweets) instead of *dagashi* (cheap nostalgic corner-store candy) and convenience-store finds?** That's a different category of subscription entirely.

For a side-by-side look at the major boxes, see our [three-box comparison](/blog/japanese-snack-box-comparison/), and for the traditional-sweets direction, our [Bokksu review](/blog/bokksu-review/).

If what you want is the *konbini* (Japanese convenience store) shelf itself — themed, complete, monthly — this is the box built for that.

## TokyoTreat FAQ

**How much does TokyoTreat cost per month?**
The single plan runs $37.50 month-to-month, falling to $32.50 per month on a 12-month term. With shipping added, expect around $52 all-in — about $4.30 per snack.

**How many snacks come in a TokyoTreat box?**
Roughly 12 to 13 items per box, plus a color guidebook explaining the month's theme. The July 2026 Akihabara box held 12 items; the May 2026 Osaka box held 13.

**Does every TokyoTreat box include a Japanese KitKat?**
Every one of the six most recent boxes included a full-size bag of a Japan-exclusive KitKat flavor. The flavor rotates with the theme — Salted Caramel for Osaka, Banana for Showa retro, Cookies and Cream for Akihabara, Strawberry Cheesecake for Okinawa.

**Can you cancel TokyoTreat anytime?**
Yes. The subscription renews automatically each month, and cancellation is available at any point. Sign up now and your first delivery is the August 2026 Okinawa box.
